# Sales Qualification Frameworks

## MEDDPICC — The Enterprise Standard

Created at PTC in 1996. Helped grow from $300M to $1B. Used by 73% of SaaS companies selling >$100K ARR. Organizations report 18% higher win rates and 24% larger deals.

### Choose Your Variant
- **MEDDIC** (6): $50K-$250K deals, simpler procurement
- **MEDDICC** (+Competition): Competitive $250K+ RFPs
- **MEDDPICC** (+Paper +Competition): Complex enterprise, formal procurement

### The 8 Elements

**M — Metrics:** Quantifiable economic benefit. 72% of deals fail because buyers don't see value.
- 0: No metrics discussed
- 1: Vague ("save time")
- 2: Estimated ("~$500K savings")
- 3: Validated by buyer ("CFO confirmed $500K annual savings")
- Red flag: Buyer can't articulate financial impact

**E — Economic Buyer:** Person with budget/PO authority. Not always highest title.
- 0: Unknown
- 1: Identified, no access
- 2: Met once
- 3: Direct relationship, validated support
- Red flag: "We need to run this by someone" late in deal

**D — Decision Criteria:** Formal/informal criteria to evaluate options.
- 0: Unknown
- 1: Assumed
- 2: Documented by buyer
- 3: You've helped shape the criteria
- Red flag: Criteria change late-stage

**D — Decision Process:** Steps to reach purchase decision.
- 0: Unknown
- 1: Vague ("a few weeks")
- 2: Documented steps
- 3: Timeline confirmed with next steps committed
- Red flag: No clear next step after meetings

**P — Paper Process:** Legal, security, procurement steps. Kills deals as often as feature gaps.
- 0: Not discussed
- 1: General understanding
- 2: Specific steps/contacts identified
- 3: Legal/procurement engaged with timeline
- Red flag: No procurement contact identified

**I — Implicate Pain:** Connect pain to business consequences (time, cost, risk, revenue).
- 0: No pain identified
- 1: Surface pain ("need better reporting")
- 2: Quantified ("losing $200K/quarter")
- 3: Connected to strategic initiative with exec sponsorship
- Red flag: Pain is "nice to have" not "must solve"

**C — Champion:** Internal advocate willing to ACT (not just agree).
- 0: No advocate
- 1: Friendly but no influence
- 2: Influential and advocates
- 3: Actively sells internally, has EB access
- Red flag: "Champion" can't get you an EB meeting. Test: ask them to act. Real champions act; others agree.

**C — Competition:** All alternatives including status quo.
- 0: Unknown
- 1: Know competitors, not positioning
- 2: Understand landscape and buyer perception
- 3: Differentiated positioning validated by champion
- Red flag: Don't know who else is being evaluated

### CRM Scoring Weights
Metrics 20%, Economic Buyer 20%, Decision+Paper 25%, Pain 15%, Champion 10%, Competition 10%. Require 70%+ before commit forecast.

## SPICED — Winning by Design

Designed for recurring revenue. Works cross-departmentally (Sales, CS, Marketing share the language).

| Element | What to Uncover | Key Questions |
|---------|----------------|---------------|
| **S — Situation** | Current state facts (not opinions): tech stack, team, processes | "What tools do you use for X?" "Walk me through your process." |
| **P — Pain** | Primary challenges and root causes | "Biggest challenge with X?" "What happens when Y fails?" |
| **I — Impact** | Quantifiable benefit of solving. Reps who master Impact sell 53% more. | "What would solving this mean?" "How would you measure success?" |
| **CE — Critical Event** | Deadline creating urgency. NOT your quota. | "Specific date you need this solved?" "What happens if not resolved?" |
| **D — Decision** | Full buying process: criteria, stakeholders, approvals | "Who else involved?" "Purchased something like this before?" |

If there are no consequences for missing the deadline, it's not a real critical event.

## CHAMP — Mid-Market Sweet Spot

Best for $25K-$100K consultative sales where you help CREATE budget.

- **CH — Challenges:** Business problem. Lead with pain, not budget.
- **A — Authority:** Decision involvement. Even non-decision-makers can be champion influencers.
- **M — Money:** Financial capacity. Comes AFTER understanding challenges.
- **P — Prioritization:** Importance vs other initiatives. The urgency filter.

## Adapting to Deal Size

| Segment | ACV | Framework | Cycle | Qualification Time |
|---------|-----|-----------|-------|--------------------|
| SMB | <$25K | BANT | <30 days | 15-20 min |
| Mid-Market | $25K-$100K | CHAMP/SPICED | 3-6 months | Discovery call(s) |
| Enterprise | $100K+ | MEDDPICC | 6-18 months | Multi-stage, artifact-based |

**Layered approach:** BANT for initial screening (5 min) -> CHAMP for discovery -> MEDDIC for deep enterprise qualification.
