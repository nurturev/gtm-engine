# Vertical-Specific GTM Knowledge

## SaaS Companies
- Buyers are sophisticated, often have own GTM expertise
- PLG common for fast time-to-value tools; sales-led for complex/high-ACV
- Vertical SaaS growing — industry-tailored > horizontal
- Sub-vertical prioritization beats trying to serve entire industry
- Bottom-funnel content chronically underinvested — prioritize case studies, comparison pages, technical how-to's
- Signal-driven outbound outperforms passive funnels

## Fintech / Financial Services
- **Sales cycles:** 6-18 months (compliance requirements)
- **Trust is #1 currency** — you're asking institutions to plug into core infrastructure
- **Compliance as table stakes:** SOC 2, PCI DSS, AML/KYC, data privacy. 93% of fintechs struggle with compliance
- **Risk officers have veto power** regardless of economic buyer approval
- **Multi-stakeholder:** CFO, CIO/CTO, Head of Risk/Compliance, Legal, Operations, Product
- **Highest CAC:** $1,450 average across all industries
- **GTM tip:** Prepare vendor docs, security certs, contract templates BEFORE first meeting. Faster compliance navigation = faster close
- Regulatory fluency is prerequisite, not differentiator

## Healthcare / Life Sciences
- **Sales cycles:** 12-24 months; only 43% close within 6 months
- **Buying committees:** Average 22 decision-makers (clinical, admin, IT, procurement)
- **HIPAA/HITECH non-negotiable.** Prepare compliance package, SOC 2, BAA template before first meeting — removes 4-8 weeks
- **Budget timing:** Q2-Q3 optimal (building next year's budgets). Q1 tough (already allocated). Q4 for urgent compliance.
- **Always-on required:** Episodic campaigns fail with 12-24 month timelines. Invest in evergreen educational content.
- **Life sciences:** Spans pharma, devices, CROs. Requires GxP familiarity. Cycles span R&D, medical, regulatory, commercial.
- ABM essential due to long cycles and high-value deals

## E-commerce / D2C
- **Buyer psychology:** Consumer-brand operators think in fast iteration, direct relationships, performance metrics (CAC, LTV, ROAS, AOV)
- **Bridge B2C and B2B:** Speak their language (ecommerce KPIs) while running structured B2B process
- **Shorter evaluation** than enterprise but still multi-stakeholder (founder, marketing, ops, finance)
- **Communities:** r/ecommerce, Shopify partner ecosystems, D2C Slack groups
- **Proof points:** Metrics-driven case studies showing revenue/conversion/retention impact
- More receptive to social channels (including TikTok, Instagram)

## Enterprise vs Mid-Market vs SMB

| Dimension | SMB (<500 emp) | Mid-Market ($50M-$1B rev) | Enterprise ($1B+ rev) |
|-----------|---------------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Sales cycle | <4 months | 4-6 months | 6-18 months |
| Decision makers | 1-3 | 4-7 | 10+ |
| GTM motion | PLG, high-velocity, digital | Semi-consultative, hybrid | Sales-led, ABM, relationship |
| CAC payback | 6-12 months | 12-18 months | 18-36 months |
| Key messaging | Quick value, ease, price | Scalability, workflow fit | Risk reduction, reputation, strategic ROI |
| Retention | Higher churn, lower expansion | Moderate | Best retention + expansion |

**Dual-motion approach (2025):** High-velocity digital for SMB/mid-market + consultative ABM for enterprise, with separate teams, metrics, and playbooks for each.

## Choosing a Vertical — Framework

1. **Start with best customers** — which vertical do they cluster in?
2. **Score verticals** by: market size, pain severity, willingness to pay, sales cycle, competitive density
3. **Go deep before wide** — own one sub-vertical before expanding
4. **Build vertical proof points** — case studies, terminology, compliance knowledge
5. **Hire from the industry** — domain expertise accelerates trust and shortens cycles
