# Modern Outbound Playbooks (2025-2026)

## The Shift: Volume to Signals

Stricter inbox enforcement killed high-volume outbound. The new playbook is signal-based, multi-channel, hyper-personalized.

**Key stats:**
- 61% of B2B buyers prefer rep-free experience
- 84% have selected a vendor BEFORE talking to a rep
- First seller to reach buyer after trigger event: 5x more likely to win
- 70% of buyer journey happens in the "Dark Funnel" (anonymous research)

## Multi-Channel Sequence Framework

**Optimal: 6-8 touchpoints across 21-27 days, 3+ channels (email, phone, LinkedIn)**

| Day | Channel | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| 1 | LinkedIn | Blank connection request (no note = higher acceptance) |
| 2 | Email | Cold email #1 (75-100 words, no meeting ask — ask for interest) |
| 4 | Phone | Call + voicemail |
| 6 | LinkedIn | Engage with their content or send message |
| 8 | Email | Follow-up with relevant insight |
| 11 | Phone | Call attempt |
| 14 | LinkedIn | Share relevant resource |
| 17 | Email | Case study or social proof |
| 20 | Phone | Follow-up call |
| 23 | Email | Final value-add email |
| 27 | — | Move to nurture sequence |

**Best practices:**
- Lead with LinkedIn (blank connection increases email/call response)
- Keep emails 75-100 words; 75 is optimal
- Don't ask for meeting in email #1 — ask for interest
- 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups; 44% of reps quit after 1
- Campaigns with 4-7 emails get 3x responses vs 1-3 emails
- Test with 200-300 prospects before tweaking
- Tier: T1 (hyper-personalized), T2 (persona-based), T3 (templated)

## Kyle Coleman's AE Outbound Framework
1. Tier accounts — top ICP only. Don't burn them with generic outreach.
2. Find warm paths first — referrals, partners, existing clients. Invite to events before pitching.
3. Research with intent — Why now (triggers), competitors as clients, shareable business cases.
4. Sequence with purpose — Day 1: Email with business case to top 2-3 decision makers. Day 3-4: Call referencing email + clear reason to talk.

Philosophy: Outbound is a long game with right accounts, not a volume game.

## Becc Holland's "Flip the Script" Framework
Core insight: If the problem was large and painful enough, buyers would already be seeking a solution. Leading with UNKNOWN problems — issues buyers didn't realize they had — expands addressable market.

**5 Personalization Buckets (from LinkedIn profiles):**
1. Self-authored content (highest response rates)
2. Shared experiences or connections
3. Company news and triggers
4. Role-specific challenges
5. Industry trends

Sequence structure: Fewer touches at front, more at end (opposite of most reps). Pattern disruption increases engagement.

## Morgan Ingram's Formula
**Intent + Outbound + Content = Pipeline**

Three pillars: Know who's ready (intent), orchestrate touches (outbound), build trust before the meeting (content).

Philosophy: "Stop running your team like an assembly line. Start running it like a creative studio that happens to sell."
