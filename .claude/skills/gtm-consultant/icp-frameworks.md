# ICP Definition Frameworks

## Framework 1: Pick 3 Attributes (Lenny Rachitsky)
Based on 20+ top B2B startups (Notion, Figma, Amplitude, Gong, Linear).

Pick exactly 3 attributes from: company size, job title, pain point, unique way of working, specific tech used, type of business, price point, geography, place the user spends time.

Key rule: Get "almost comically narrow." 5 paying customers who look the same = good sign. ICP is iterative, not one-time.

## Framework 2: Five Questions (a16z)
1. Which current customers get the most value from your product?
2. What traits do your best customers share?
3. What recurring objections come from lost deals?
4. What expansion patterns do you see in successful accounts?
5. What market signals indicate readiness to buy?

## Framework 3: Firmographics + Triggers + Macro Trends (TK Kader)
- Firmographics: industry, size, revenue, geography
- Triggers: funding, new hires, regulatory changes, competitive losses
- Macro Trends: market forces making solutions more relevant now

## Framework 4: Data-Driven 7-Step Process

Step 1 — Audit best customers. Export 12 months closed-won. Filter LTV > 3x CAC. Analyze win rates, deal sizes, time-to-close.

Step 2 — Define firmographic criteria. Company size, revenue, industry, geography, maturity.

Step 3 — Map technographics. Use Clearbit, ZoomInfo, BuiltWith to find tech stack patterns.

Step 4 — Identify behavioral signals & pain points. From sales recordings and customer interviews. Must be quantifiable ("reduce churn by 20%").

Step 5 — Build weighted scoring system:
- Revenue fit: 40%
- Technographics: 20%
- Behavioral signals: 20%
- Intent/triggers: 20%

Step 6 — Operationalize. Set must-haves, positive indicators, caution flags, disqualifiers.

Step 7 — Refine quarterly. ICP drift leads to longer cycles and lower win rates.

## ICP Scoring Rubric

| Category | Weight | Criteria | Score |
|----------|--------|----------|-------|
| Firmographics | 30-40% | Industry match, company size, geography, business model | 1-10 each |
| Technographics | 15-20% | CRM used, complementary tools, tech sophistication | 1-10 each |
| Behavioral | 20-25% | Website visits, content downloads, engagement | 1-10 each |
| Intent/Triggers | 20-25% | Funding, key hires, competitor switch, job postings | 1-10 each |

## ICP Tiers

- **Tier A (80-100):** Fully personalized engagement, dedicated rep
- **Tier B (50-79):** Warm nurture, semi-personalized campaigns
- **Tier C (25-49):** Automated coverage, programmatic outreach
- **Below 25:** Negative ICP — actively exclude

## Key Stats
- Organizations with strong ICP scoring: 68% higher win rates
- Tier A accounts: 1.8x higher ACV, 15-20% shorter cycles
- ICP-aligned leads: 25%+ lower CAC, 20%+ higher renewal rates

## Negative ICP (Equally Important)
Define who to actively exclude. Prevents wasted cycles and protects win rates. Common negative signals: too small (can't afford), too large (24-month procurement), wrong industry vertical, tech stack incompatible.

## Expansion ICP
Which existing customers are most likely to buy more? Different attributes than acquisition ICP. Look for: growing team size, increasing product usage, new department adoption, approaching feature limits.
