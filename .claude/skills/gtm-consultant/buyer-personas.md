# Buyer Persona & Psychology Frameworks

## JTBD + Persona Hybrid

Personas reveal WHO buys; Jobs-to-Be-Done explains WHY. Use both together.

### Bob Moesta's Demand-Side Sales Framework
Interview 10 recent buyers (30-60 min each) to uncover the 3-5 distinct "struggling moments" covering 90% of your market.

**Four Forces of Progress:**
1. **Push** — dissatisfaction with current state
2. **Pull** — attraction of new solution
3. **Anxiety** — fear of change ("what if it's worse?")
4. **Habit** — comfort with status quo ("it's good enough")

Key question: "What was going on that made you say today's the day?" Ignore features — focus on the struggling moment.

### April Dunford's Positioning Framework
5 components: competitive alternatives, key differentiators, value delivered, customers who care most, market context.

Critical insight: The biggest competitor in B2B is INDECISION — "not to decide" kills 40-60% of pipeline.

## Decision-Making Unit (DMU)

Modern B2B: 6-10 stakeholders (Gartner), up to 13 (Forrester). 38% include CEOs. 79% require CFO approval.

| Role | What They Care About | How to Engage |
|------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Champion** | Solving their problem, looking good internally | Give slides, data, case studies to sell when you're not in the room |
| **Economic Buyer** | ROI, risk, strategic alignment, budget | Business outcomes, TCO analysis, payback period |
| **Technical Evaluator** | Integrations, security, performance | Technical docs, sandbox access, architecture reviews |
| **End User** | Ease of use, workflow fit, daily experience | Trials, workflow demos, migration support |
| **Blocker** | Risk, competing priorities, politics | Address concerns directly with targeted info |
| **Influencer** | Reputation, best practices, peer validation | Case studies, analyst reports, peer references |

Build stakeholder maps with names, roles, priorities, and influence levels for every deal.

## Buying Triggers & Timing

| Trigger | Why It Signals Intent | Best Timing |
|---------|----------------------|-------------|
| Leadership change | New execs reassess vendors, want quick wins | 30-60 days post-appointment |
| Funding round | Capital to spend, building infrastructure | Within 1-2 weeks |
| Hiring surge | Roles reveal strategic priorities | Within a week |
| Tech migration | Integration opportunities, vendor evaluation | When publicly announced |
| Competitor incident | Outage, breach, pricing controversy | Immediately |
| Compliance deadline | Creates urgency | 3-6 months before |
| M&A / IPO | Strategic shift, budget available | Post-announcement |
| Contract renewal | Existing vendor under review | 90 days before |

## Emotional Drivers in B2B

Google/CEB: B2B customers are MORE emotionally connected to vendors than B2C consumers. Career risk influences 74% of purchase decisions.

**Core fears:** Wrong choice damaging career, being left behind, change management failure, vendor failing to deliver.

**Core aspirations:** Professional advancement ("you'll get promoted"), being seen as innovative, building something meaningful, reducing workload.

**Practical implication:** Logic justifies emotional decisions. Address both the rational case (ROI) and emotional reality (career safety, professional pride).

## Chris Walker's Demand Gen Framework

Three buckets:
1. **Pipeline marketing** — bottom-funnel ABM, field marketing, sales enablement
2. **Capture demand** — find in-market buyers, get them to meeting fast
3. **Create demand** — educate in dark funnel (podcasts, communities, social). Requires different metrics and patience.

80% of B2B buying journey happens in "dark social" (unmeasurable by attribution). Add "how did you hear about us?" to forms.

## Sangram Vajre's TEAM Framework

**T.E.A.M.:** Target (account lists via firmographic + technographic + intent), Engage (personalized interactions), Activate (enable sales with nurtured leads), Measure (pipeline progress, not MQL volume).

**Flip My Funnel:** Identify > Expand > Engage > Advocate. Happy customers drive more revenue than new leads.

Key: "Stop counting leads and start making leads count."
